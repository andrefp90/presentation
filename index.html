Zulu

Zulu is a 3-year-old male cheetah, he leaves in Reavyfeex sanctuary with his partner Sansa.The cheetah is the fastest land mammal they can go from 0 to 60 miles an hour in only three seconds. Most wild cheetahs can be found in eastern and southwestern Africa. There are only 7,000 to 10,000 cheetahs in the wild.To learn more about cheetahs you can visit National Geographic web page.
Photo taken by: Andrea Bertello

Kenya

Meet Kenya one of our Howler monkeys at Reavyfeex,we are the only sanctuary where they have reproduced successfully. Howlers or Saraguatos are New World monkeys found in tropical Central and South America.Their howls can be heard up to three miles away some people say that their howl is very similar to the sound a Jaguar does. For more information, you can visit National Geographic web page.
Photo taken by: Andrea Bertello

Congo

Congo is one of the male jaguar residents at Reavyfeex along with Goliath, Matika, Dakota and Maculi. The jaguar is the largest of the South American big cats you can find them from Central America to South America, but they are in great extinction.They are very similar to the leopard, but the difference between the two of them is that they have rosettes inside their spots. For more information, you can visit National Geographic web page.
Photo taken by: Andrea Bertello

Arusha

Arusha is one of the lynxes that lives in Reavyfeex. Lynxes are a solitary cat that originates from North America, Europe and Asia. For more information, please visit National Geographic web page.
Photo taken by: Andrea Bertello

Jambo

Jambo arrived at Reavyfeex when he was five days old after being bottle fed every 3 hours he grew up to be this magnificent male tiger that weighs 450 lbs and is now the father of Savannah and Sherkan. Tigers are the largest members of the cat family, because of trophy hunting deforestation and traditional Chinese medicine there are less than 2,500 in the wild. For more information, please visit National Geographic web page.
Photo taken by: Andrea Bertello

Nakuru

Nakuru is one of the white tigers at Reavyfeex he arrived this year after living his whole life in a circus. The fist day Nakuru arrived at the sanctuary, it was a great joy seeing him for the first time in grass and water he was so happy.
